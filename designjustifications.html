The User Testimonials

User Testing reports

Eva
The test person for this usertest was relatively unacquainted with common web design, meaning that she had difficulties with anticipating what content could be included in the individual sub-headings. What made the test even more difficult for her was a language barrier, she did not always understand the meaning of these headings. However, she expressed that her difficulties with navigating the website were not due to bad web-design, but more because of the abovementioned impairments. She did not claim that the website was convoluted or structured badly, in fact she said that she quite liked the design of the site.

Sabine
The test person had no trouble navigating through the prototype website. However, it was slightly unclear that there was the option to scroll (up and down), but eventually she discovered this feature but that was due to the fact that it was a prototype made in Adobe XD and that it was not an actual published website.

Joëlle
The test person again had no trouble navigating through the prototype. He sometimes was a bit unsure where to go, but his first guess was always right. He stated in the end that he thought it was a clear website without any troubles.

Tim
The test person had no trouble navigating the prototype website. The only thing that was harder to find were the testimonials and she felt like they weren’t in the most logical place.

Liam
The test person had no trouble navigating the prototype website. They were a bit unsure once of where to go to find the testimonials, mostly due too not knowing what they where, but found them within 3 clicks.

Guy
The test person had no issue with finding the first three tasks, by making quick use of the navigating bar. He had more trouble with finding the testimonials, for he needed to scroll down. After the testimonials the last three tasks were easy to find in a few clicks.
